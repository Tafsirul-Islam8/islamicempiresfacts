Rashidun Caliphate (632–661 CE) 📜 Overview The Rashidun Caliphate was the first Islamic state established after the death of Prophet Muhammad in 632 CE. It was led by four righteous caliphs, known as the Rashidun (Rightly Guided). This period is considered one of the most important eras in Islamic history due to its rapid expansion, strong leadership, and establishment of governance based on justice and Islamic principles. 👑 The Four Rashidun Caliphs Abu Bakr (632–634 CE) First caliph of Islam United Arabia after the Ridda Wars Preserved stability after the Prophet’s death Umar ibn al-Khattab (634–644 CE) Expanded the empire massively Conquered Persia, Egypt, and parts of Byzantium Established administrative systems Uthman ibn Affan (644–656 CE) Standardized the Qur'an into one official version Continued expansion Faced internal unrest Ali ibn Abi Talib (656–661 CE) Cousin and son-in-law of the Prophet Faced civil wars (First Fitna) Known for wisdom and justice 🌍 Expansion of the Empire During the Rashidun period, the Islamic empire expanded rapidly: 🏜️ Arabian Peninsula (fully unified) 🇪🇬 Egypt 🇮🇶 Iraq 🇮🇷 Persia (Sassanid Empire defeated) 🇸🇾 Syria and Levant This expansion weakened two major powers: Byzantine Empire Sasanian Empire ⚔️ Important Events Ridda Wars (632–633) – Reunified Arabia Battle of Yarmouk (636) – Defeated Byzantines Battle of Qadisiyyah (636) – Defeated Persians First Fitna (656–661) – First Islamic civil war 🏛️ Administration & Achievements Established Islamic governance system Introduced Bait al-Mal (treasury) Built justice-based administration Spread Islam across vast regions 📉 End of the Rashidun Caliphate The Rashidun Caliphate ended in 661 CE after the assassination of Ali ibn Abi Talib. It was followed by the Umayyad Caliphate.
